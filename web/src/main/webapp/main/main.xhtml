Recommended browsers for use with #{appInfo.applicationProperties.map['screensaver.ui.application_name']} are (TBD)
  • Use "Browse Libraries" to view the set of small molecule and RNAi libraries available for use at the Screening Facility. For each library, you can view descriptive information on the Library Viewer page. From that page, you can navigate to the Wells Browser page, to browse the contents of each library well and reagent information including structures, SMILES strings, and links to PubChem, ChemBank, and GenBank.
    • Use "Find Wells" or "Find Reagents" to search for information on library reagents, using library plate/well designations or vendor-assigned reagent identifiers, respectively. Use "My Screens" to view your own screens and result data, and "Browse Screens" to view basic information about other screens performed at ICCB-L. For your own screens, you can browse, filter, sort, and export your result data, and compare it to other shared screen results and studies. You can also view each screening data plate with a heat map viewer and export the screen result data as a spreadsheet (xls, csv, or sdf).
    • If your screen result data is not shown, please contact David Wrobel and we will make it a priority to import your data.
    • The amount of screen results visible from any screens other than your own is dependent upon your small molecule and/or RNAi data sharing level. Please contact Jennifer SmithÂ  if you have any questions.
  • Use "Browse Studies" to view the studies that have been added to Screensaver. Studies associate annotations with library reagents.
  • Use "Browse Lab Activities" to view the activities you have performed at .
  • Use "Data Downloads" to download SD files for all libraries, either as a single file or as individual library files.
  • Note: the process of downloading images of compound structures into Excel is somewhat slow. For example, it may take ~10 minutes to generate an Excel file containing 1000 structures. If you would like to download large numbers of images, please consider dividing your export request into several smaller batches of images. You are also welcome to contact the Screensaver informatics team to generate a custom report for you
